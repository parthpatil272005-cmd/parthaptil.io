TIFAN 2025

Overview

TIFAN 2025 was my first major engineering project during my second year of college. We restarted the entire design due to previous prototype failures in seed handling, alignment, and mechanical synchronization.

Design Breakdown

Picking System: Lifts seedlings without damaging roots.
Conveying System: Transfers seedlings smoothly.
Planting System: Drops seedlings at consistent spacing.

Mechanisms I Designed

Geneva Mechanism for Conveyor Indexing
Ensured precise rotational steps and synced movement

✔ Accurate indexing
✔ Smooth synchronization
✔ Reduced backlash
Lead Screw System (Vertical + Horizontal Movement)
Designed dual-screw motion using NEMA 17 & 23 motors.

✔ High accuracy movement
✔ Durable in dusty field conditions
✔ Controlled gripper travel

Electronic System Selection

✔ PLC for timing synchronization
✔ Stepper motors (NEMA 17/23)
✔ Optical sensors for position tracking
✔ Relay modules for actuation control

Challenges & Redesign

Main Issues:
✔ Seedling damage during pickup
✔ Conveyor misalignment
✔ Timing mismatch
✔ Jamming during continuous operation
Fixes Implemented:
✔ Redesigned gripper geometry
✔ Improved conveyor alignment
✔ PLC feedback loop
✔ Lead screw replacement for durability

My Role

I contributed to mechanical design, system synchronization, and electronic selection. My major responsibilities included:

✔ Designing Geneva mechanism for conveyor indexing
✔ Developing lead-screw based vertical and horizontal motion
✔ Selecting NEMA stepper motors & electronic components
✔ Precision tuning of pickup mechanism to reduce seedling damage
✔ Testing & analyzing system timing via PLC feedback
✔ Troubleshooting mechanical failures in field conditions

I worked closely with seniors and faculty to refine the system until testing showed stable and reliable performance.
