EV Go-Kart

Overview

The EV Go-Kart was my first practical introduction to electric vehicle technology. I joined the Electrical Department in my 1st year and continued leading electrical tasks through 2nd year. During this time, I learned EV circuitry, motor controllers, battery systems, safety protocols, and software-based tuning for real-world competition use.

The goal of the project was to build a high-performance and reliable electric kart capable of acceleration runs, endurance races, braking tests, and dynamic events. I contributed to electrical design, integration, troubleshooting, and system optimization.

Achievements

Regenerative Braking Integration
Successfully added regenerative braking, allowing the kart to recharge during deceleration, increasing run time and reducing mechanical brake load.
Boost Mode Development
Programmed a temporary high-power output mode for quick acceleration & overtaking in competition.
Controller Optimization
Tuned current limits, torque curves, and throttle mapping to increase efficiency and endurance.
Electrical Reliability
Designed a clean wiring harness that passed scrutineering, vibration tests, and thermal checks.
Chassis & Electrical Integration
Ensured safe and efficient routing of wiring, controller placement, battery mounting, and cooling considerations while coordinating with mechanical team.
